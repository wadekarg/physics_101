The Scientific Method

The systematic approach scientists use to investigate natural phenomena and build reliable knowledge.

Theory

The scientific method is the backbone of all physics and natural science. It is a systematic, iterative process designed to minimize bias and produce reliable knowledge about the natural world. The method typically begins with observation — noticing a phenomenon or pattern in nature. From observation, a scientist formulates a hypothesis, which is a testable, falsifiable prediction about how nature behaves. A good hypothesis must be specific enough that an experiment could potentially prove it wrong. For example, "heavier objects fall faster than lighter objects" is a testable hypothesis that Galileo famously investigated.

Once a hypothesis is stated, the scientist designs and conducts a controlled experiment. In a well-designed experiment, only one variable (the independent variable) is changed at a time, while all other conditions (the controlled variables) are kept constant. The outcome being measured is the dependent variable. After collecting data, the scientist performs analysis — looking for patterns, computing statistics, and determining whether the results support or refute the hypothesis. If the hypothesis is consistently supported across many experiments by independent researchers, it may eventually become part of a broader theory — a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of nature.

An essential part of the scientific method is peer review and reproducibility. Scientists publish their methods and results so that others can scrutinize and repeat the experiments. A result that cannot be reproduced is not considered reliable. Physics has thrived because of this rigorous culture: from Newton's laws to Einstein's relativity, every major theory has survived decades of experimental testing. Remember that a scientific theory is never "proven" in an absolute sense — it is always open to revision in light of new evidence. This self-correcting nature is what makes science so powerful.
